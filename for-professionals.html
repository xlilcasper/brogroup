For Professionals

If you work with men who have experienced sexual trauma — as a therapist, counselor, physician, chaplain, case manager, or any other helping role — BRO can be a powerful complement to the care you provide.

What We Are

Our Mission for Professionals

BRO exists to fill a gap that clinical care alone often cannot. We offer something therapists and counselors cannot replicate: brotherhood. Men who have been through it, supporting other men who are going through it.

We are not a replacement for professional mental health treatment. We are an supplement — a bridge between sessions, a community that extends beyond the office.

Many BRO members are referred by therapists, parole officers, victim advocates, chaplains, and physicians. Some men would never walk into a therapist's office but will walk into a peer support group. BRO gives you another door to offer.

The Format

How BRO Meetings Work

BRO meetings are intentionally simple. This is by design — simplicity lowers barriers and keeps the focus on connection, not process.

1

Opening (5 minutes)

A brief, consistent opening reminds everyone of the confidentiality commitment, the non-judgmental space, and the right to pass.

2

Open Sharing (45“60 min)

Men share as they feel comfortable. Some say nothing. Some share briefly. Some go deeper. There is no structure imposed on what anyone "should" talk about.

3

Closing (2“3 minutes)

A brief closing statement. Men leave when they are ready — no obligation to stay for the full meeting.

Core Principles

  • No cross-talk. No interrupting, fixing, or advice-giving. Each man's experience is his own.
  • Confidentiality. What is said at BRO stays at BRO. Every member agrees to this.
  • No pressure. Men can attend any number of meetings before sharing anything.
  • Listening counts. Showing up and listening is a full form of participation.
  • No ideology. No religious, political, or organizational agenda. BRO is strictly about peer support.

Referral Guidance

Who Is a Good Fit for BRO?

Good candidates for BRO:

  • Adult men who have experienced sexual abuse or assault at any point in their lives
  • Men who have plateaued in individual therapy and would benefit from peer connection
  • Men who resist clinical settings but are open to a peer community
  • Men who feel isolated and want to know they are not alone
  • Men in the maintenance phase of trauma recovery
  • Men who want to give back by supporting others

When to recommend additional support:

  • Active suicidal ideation — please ensure crisis resources are in place first
  • Active substance use disorder requiring detox or residential treatment
  • Current involvement in a legal case where testimony may be affected
  • Psychotic symptoms or active severe mental health crisis

BRO facilitators are not clinicians. We are trained peer supporters. We will refer a member to professional care if we believe they need it.
